Caregiver's Guidelines

Cleaning and Disinfecting:

Clear and disinfect the kitchen countertops, including the island, at least once per shift.

Proper Storage:

Store all supplies and food in their correct locations (kitchen, pantry, or dining room shelves). The kitchen and dining room tables are NOT for storage. Please do not pile items on these tables.

Familiarization:

Take a few minutes to familiarize yourself with the pantry and storage areas. If unsure about where an item is stored, ask John or leave the items on the island.

Reporting Damages:

Report any damages or broken items (tableware, appliances, etc.) to John before the end of your shift.

Grocery List Management:

Add items to the Alexa grocery list if they are closed to empty or used up. Check the pantry for extra stock before adding to Alexa.

Handling Food:

Throw away old, spoiled, or inedible food. Remember, if you wouldn’t eat it, John won’t either. Throw it away. Discard older items in the refrigerator when replaced with new groceries. Discard leftover food unless instructed otherwise. Leftover food often goes bad and takes up space. If John doesn't eat it, it should be thrown away or given to the dog. Period. Store unopened, shelf-stable grocery items on the pantry shelves. Organize items before adding to Alexa. Put items away where they are currently stored.

Groceries Handling:

Store freezer items in the outside freezer, kitchen freezer, or prepare to eat soon. Ask if unsure. Store refrigerator items properly and discard expired items before storing new ones. If a cold item is left out by accident or for convenience, put it in the refrigerator or freezer when you are cleaning up.

Kitchen Tools:

Store kitchen tools in their specific places. Clean pans and pots properly before putting them away. If you don’t know where to store an item, ask John.

Checklist Before Finishing Your Shift:

  • Is there anything on the table that should be put away?
  • Is there anything in the sink that's blocking the garbage disposal?
  • Is there any food or fly-making material in the trash can that should be in the garbage disposal?
